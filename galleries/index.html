Galleries

Orientation Gallery

The Orientation Gallery introduces people to the MIM and how musical instruments are a reflection of culture and human nature. Musical instruments in this gallery are generally organized by a metaphorical theme, their common use genre, or typological classification.

Geographic Gallery

The Geographic Gallery displays instruments through a cultural lens. The Geographic is divided most broadly by continent and then into display walls by countries. Larger countries like China even have displays dedicated to specific regions within the nation. Over 200 countries are represented.

Experience Gallery

The Experience Gallery is designed to engage visitors in a hands-on exploration of musical instruments.

Artist Gallery

Displays in the Artist Gallery highlight influential artists and the instruments they have played.

Mechanical Gallery

The Mechanical Gallery showcases instruments that produce sound via mechanical means, such as player pianos, orchestrions, and music boxes.

Other Galleries

Beyond those shown here, the MIM also has a few other galleries. The most significant is the STEM Gallery. This gallery highlights how science and technology impact the creation of music. Unfortunately, I have not visited the MIM with general admission since this was added. There is also the Encore Gallery, which is a counterpart of the Experience Gallery that is designed for younger children. Furthermore, the MIM also has limited exhibits, which will depend on time of visit.
